Once you install and configure the app, it runs in the background. It waits for you to join a meeting.

The app stays invisible to other people. If you share your screen in a meeting app like Zoom or Microsoft Teams, your viewers only see your work. They do not see the assistant overlay.

The software listens to the audio during your calls. It transcribes the conversation into text. You can see this text in the floating window. The assistant suggests relevant information based on the things people say. 

### Privacy
All processing stays local where possible. The assistant does not record your screen for others. It only uses the visuals for the internal processing of the AI model. 

## 🛡️ Safety and Troubleshooting

If the assistant does not launch, check your computer settings. 

- Ensure you use Windows 10 or Windows 11.
- Make sure your microphone is active and the application has permission to access it.
- Check that your API key remains valid and has enough credits or funds.
- Close other apps that use the microphone if the assistant struggles to hear the audio.

If the floating window disappears, look at the taskbar near the clock. Click the icon to bring the window back to the front of your screen.

The app processes audio for your immediate benefit. It does not store recordings on a central server.

No. The window exists as an overlay on your machine. Screen sharing software ignores this layer by design. 

**What happens if my API key fails?**
The assistant will pause its work. You will see a notification in the settings menu. Simply update the key to resume.

**Does it work on laptops?**
Yes. Any computer running a modern version of Windows will support the application.

**How do I hide the window?**
Click the minimize button in the corner of the window. You can reopen it at any time from the system tray.
